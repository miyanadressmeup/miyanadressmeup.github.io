AVOID CRACKED HEELS THIS WINTER 

When we go and about in the summers, dressed in breezy clothes and strappy sandals, our feet are spotlessly clean and well taken care of! Yet, as we step into the winter months, we tend to neglect our feet, when they disappear into thick socks and shoes. What we donâ€™t realize is that the cold dries out our feet, even more, resulting in cracked, bleeding heels if not nourished properly. To solve this beauty woe, follow these tips and keep your feet soft, supple and moisturized through the winter months:
- Soak your feet in warm water with salt, lemon for 10 minutes twice a week at home. After soaking, gently scrub off dry skin.


- Post scrubbing your feet, use a foot cream immediately to pamper the feet. If you have really dry skin, then look for specialised creams for feet, which are thicker than usual face creams. Also for best results, use a foot cream every night before bed.

- A massage is great not only for dry, cracked soles but also to remove all fatigue from your feet, leaving your feet relaxed. A massage with coconut oil, ghee, almond oil or sesame oil will get the circulation going in your feet and help trap in the moisture.


- If you still notice dry heels, then use a petroleum jelly, which can do wonders to your cracked heels. Massage it on clean feet and slip on socks during bedtime. This will help to lock in the moisture.
